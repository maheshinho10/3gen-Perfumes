# Luxury Brand Skill — 3GEN Brand Identity & Voice

## Brand Overview

**Brand Name:** 3GEN  
**Category:** Luxury Niche Perfumery  
**Positioning:** Ultra-premium, artisanal, exclusive  
**Target Audience:** Discerning individuals who seek identity through scent — not trends

---

## Brand Essence

### The Five Pillars of 3GEN

| Pillar | Expression |
|---|---|
| **Luxurious** | Every detail communicates premium quality |
| **Mysterious** | Intrigue before revelation — the scent tells a story |
| **Modern** | Contemporary aesthetics, never dated or nostalgic |
| **Elite** | Not accessible to all — earned, not given |
| **Timeless** | No seasonal campaigns — built to last decades |

### Brand Personality
- Speaks quietly but commands attention
- Does not explain itself — it invites discovery
- Treats the customer as a connoisseur, never a consumer
- Refined, never ostentatious
- Sensual, never explicit

---

## Brand Voice

### Tone of Voice
- **Written tone:** Poetic, minimal, evocative
- **Never:** Salesy, urgent, casual, or trend-chasing
- **Always:** Measured, intentional, weighted

### Copywriting Principles
```
✓ "A singular expression of presence."
✓ "Worn once. Remembered forever."
✓ "Crafted for those who require no introduction."
✓ "The art of the invisible."

✗ "Buy 2, get 1 free!"
✗ "Our best-selling fragrance!"
✗ "Shop the new collection now!"
✗ "Limited time offer"
```

### Headline Style
- Short — one to five words maximum
- Fragment sentences preferred over complete ones
- Lowercase headings can feel more intimate
- Avoid exclamation marks — confidence needs no punctuation

### Product Description Voice
```
Not: "This perfume has notes of oud and rose. Perfect for evenings."
But: "A slow burn. Oud smoke drifting through cold marble corridors. 
     Rose, stripped of sweetness. The trace you leave in a room you've already left."
```

## Visual Identity

### Color System
| Role | Color | Hex |
|---|---|---|
| Primary background | Matte black | `#0A0A0A` |
| Elevated surface | Deep charcoal | `#1A1A1A` |
| Subtle border | Smoke | `#2A2A2A` |
| Primary accent | Rich gold | `#C9A84C` |
| Hover accent | Light gold | `#E2C97E` |
| Subdued accent | Muted gold | `#8B7340` |
| Primary text | Ivory | `#F5F0E8` |
| Secondary text | Dim ivory | `#C8BFA8` |

### Typography Identity
- **Display type:** Cormorant Garamond — Thin, Light, Light Italic
- **Brand mark:** DM Sans — Medium, tracked wide
- **Body:** Inter — Light (300), Regular (400)
- **Labels:** DM Sans — Medium (500), uppercase, 0.25em letter-spacing

### Photography Direction
- **Mood:** Cinematic, moody, low-key lighting
- **Color grade:** Desaturated with gold highlights — never vivid
- **Subjects:** Extreme close-up product, atmospheric environments, abstract textures
- **Never:** White studio backgrounds, flat lay on marble, lifestyle clichés

### Texture & Material Language
- Matte black glass
- Brushed gold metal
- Cold stone — marble, obsidian
- Soft leather and aged velvet
- Smoke and atmospheric haze

## Website Emotional Journey

The 3GEN website should move the visitor through these states:

```
[Arrival]       → Awe — the hero demands attention
[Discovery]     → Curiosity — they want to know more
[Immersion]     → Desire — they feel the brand world
[Connection]    → Identity — "this is who I am"
[Conviction]    → Inevitability — purchasing feels like recognition
```

### Per-Section Emotional Goals

| Section | Goal |
|---|---|
| Hero | Awe, intrigue, "what is this?" |
| Brand Story | Respect, understanding of craft |
| Collection | Desire, aesthetic pleasure |
| Ingredients | Trust, appreciation for quality |
| Testimonials | Social proof without neediness |
| Newsletter | Privilege of being part of something |
| Footer | Completion, quiet confidence |

## Brand Consistency Rules

- Never use stock photos — only editorial or custom photography direction
- Never use clipart, icons with rounded strokes, or cartoonish illustration
- Never use humor or self-deprecating copy
- Never use countdown timers, sale banners, or urgency messaging
- Never show prices without context — frame value before revealing cost
- Always make the product feel like it has a soul, not just a formula
- Always write as if the brand has existed for 50 years, even if it hasn't

---

## Naming Conventions

### Product Name Patterns
- Single-word names: Obsidian, Volta, Éther, Nox, Cendres
- Two-word poetic: Dark Accord, Still Air, Last Light
- Never descriptive names: "Fresh Ocean Breeze", "Spicy Oriental"

### Collection Names
- Seasons of darkness: The Dark Accord Collection, The Ash Collection
- Elemental: Mineral, Smoke, Resin, Vapor
- Atmospheric: The Threshold Series
